UPDATED PROSECUTION GUIDELINES FOR DRUG-RELATED OFFENSES

I. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This memorandum establishes updated guidelines for the prosecution of drug-related offenses in Los Santos, emphasizing a balanced approach that prioritizes public safety while supporting rehabilitation and addressing the root causes of drug-related crimes.

II. CLASSIFICATION OF DRUG OFFENSES

Drug offenses shall be classified into the following categories for prosecution purposes:

  • Category A - Simple Possession: Possession of controlled substances for personal use without intent to distribute
  • Category B - Possession with Intent: Possession of controlled substances with clear intent to distribute
  • Category C - Distribution/Sale: Active distribution or sale of controlled substances
  • Category D - Manufacturing/Trafficking: Large-scale manufacturing or trafficking operations

III. PROSECUTION PRIORITIES

A. Category A Offenses (Simple Possession)

Priority Level: Low - Diversion Preferred

  • Consider pre-trial diversion programs
  • Prioritize treatment over incarceration
  • Evaluate defendant's history and circumstances
  • Coordinate with social services and treatment programs
B. Category B Offenses (Possession with Intent)

Priority Level: Medium - Case-by-Case Evaluation

  • Assess quantity and circumstances
  • Consider plea agreements with treatment requirements
  • Evaluate defendant's criminal history
  • Focus on disrupting local distribution networks
C. Category C & D Offenses (Distribution/Manufacturing/Trafficking)

Priority Level: High - Full Prosecution

  • Pursue maximum penalties allowed by law
  • Coordinate with federal agencies when applicable
  • Focus on asset forfeiture and financial investigations
  • Prioritize cases involving violence or weapons

IV. SPECIAL CONSIDERATIONS

A. Juvenile Offenders

All juvenile drug cases shall be handled with special consideration for rehabilitation and education. Prosecutors shall work closely with juvenile services to develop appropriate intervention strategies.

B. Repeat Offenders

While considering criminal history, prosecutors shall evaluate whether previous offenses were driven by addiction and whether the defendant has attempted treatment.

C. Public Safety Concerns

Cases involving drug activity near schools, parks, or residential areas shall receive heightened attention and consideration for enhanced penalties.

V. COLLABORATION REQUIREMENTS

Prosecutors shall maintain active coordination with:

  • Los Santos Police Department Drug Unit
  • Treatment and rehabilitation programs
  • Probation and parole services
  • Community outreach organizations
  • Mental health professionals

VI. EFFECTIVE DATE

These guidelines take effect immediately and supersede all previous drug prosecution guidelines. All pending cases shall be reviewed under these new guidelines within 30 days.
